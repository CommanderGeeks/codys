Cody's Restaurant - Community Dining Initiative

Scaling the Agora Model: Tech-Enabled, Pre-Order Community Dining

4-Week Rotating Menu

High-volume production optimized for tilt skillets & steam kettles | 1,000 portions per service

Menu Design Principles

Production Efficiency:
  • Base + Protein + Sauce format
  • Batch cooking in steam kettles
  • Tilt skillet for proteins
  • Pre-portioned at $4.61 food cost
Dietary Coverage:
  • Always 1 Vegan + GF option (300 portions)
  • Always 1 Animal protein option (700 portions)
  • Global cuisines for variety
  • Balanced nutrition in every meal

4-Week Cycle Summary

Menu Rotation Benefits

Variety Without Complexity:
  • 112 unique dishes over 4 weeks
  • Same production methods throughout
  • Consistent portion sizes & costs
  • Predictable ingredient ordering
Operational Advantages:
  • Bulk purchasing power
  • Standardized prep procedures
  • Cross-utilization of ingredients
  • Simplified staff training

This menu system proves that high-volume community dining doesn't mean compromising on quality or variety. By using professional equipment efficiently and maintaining consistent production methods, we can deliver restaurant-quality meals at $6.99 while supporting our community with free meals funded by the operation itself.

Production Strategy

🍲 Steam Kettle Items

Perfect for:

  • All curries & stews
  • Braised meats (bourguignon, carnitas)
  • Rice & grain cooking
  • Sauces in 40L batches

🔥 Tilt Skillet Items

Ideal for:

  • Searing proteins (chicken, beef)
  • Stir-fries at scale
  • Browning for stews
  • Crispy elements

⚖️ Portion Control

Standard serving:

  • 6oz protein/legumes
  • 8oz starch/grain
  • 4oz vegetables
  • 2oz sauce/dressing

Menu Rotation Notes

Weeks 2-4 Variations

Week 2: Switches protein options (Chicken → Pork on Monday, Salmon → Cod on Tuesday)

Week 3: Seasonal vegetables rotate (Summer: zucchini/tomatoes, Winter: squash/roots)

Week 4: Sauce variations (Thai Green → Red curry, Butter Chicken → Tikka Masala)

This creates a 28-unique-day cycle while maintaining production efficiency and dietary balance.

Rotation Philosophy

This schedule embodies the community-focused values of Cody's. By having three equal-rank chefs, we eliminate hierarchy and create shared ownership of quality and success. The 14-hour ON days ensure continuity from morning prep through evening service, while the rotation prevents any single person from burning out.

Closing every second Sunday isn't just about rest—it's about recognizing that sustainable food service requires sustainable employment. With $100K chef salaries and $60K cook salaries, we're investing in professionals who can build careers, not just fill shifts.

Thesis Connection: From Agora to Cody's

Building on your Master's thesis research at UBC, Cody's represents the evolution of the Agora model from volunteer-based to professional operations. The core insights remain: removing stigma through universal design, creating community through shared dining, and achieving sustainability through integrated revenue models.

Where Agora proved students would embrace affordable, community-focused dining, Cody's scales this insight with technology and professional operations to serve broader communities while maintaining the mission of accessible, dignified food access for all.
